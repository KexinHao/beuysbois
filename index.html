beuys bois is a new art collective that teamed up with existing LGBTQ organisation The Hang-Out 070 to celebrate the LGBTQ arts in the Hague. Together we aim to make The Hague a little bit more queer.

beuys bois​ was born out of great frustration. As artists with diverse and underrepresented backgrounds attending an art school that focuses on Western forms of art, alienation became a painful part of our practice. Parts of our cultural and societal inheritance were ignored or revised. The alienation we feel is a slice in the pie of greater societal problems. On the smallest level, being fundamentally misunderstood by a mentor may cause pain, on the largest level there are segments in the population that permanently live in economic, cultural and societal alienation. We are united to imagine a different kind of institution. An institution that dares to be radical about thought and theory.

We present a space where theory becomes practice, where people who experience alienation do not have to sit with the pain. We draw our inspiration from Queer Theory. The core concepts that guide our​ explorations are non-essentialism,​ ​non-normative and​ unfixing.
